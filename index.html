Egyptian Gods and Mythology

Interesting Facts About Egyptian Gods

Ra:

Ra was the sun god and one of the most important deities in ancient Egypt. He was believed to travel across the sky during the day and journey through the underworld at night.

Anubis:

Anubis was the god of mummification and the afterlife. He was depicted as a man with a jackal's head and was responsible for guiding souls to the afterlife.

Osiris:

Osiris was the god of the dead and resurrection. He was considered the ruler of the underworld and symbolized eternal life and rebirth.

Horus:

Horus was the god of the sky and kingship. He was often depicted as a falcon or a man with a falcon's head and was associated with protection and power.

Isis:

Isis was the goddess of magic, motherhood, and fertility. She was one of the most revered deities and was known for her devotion to her husband Osiris and son Horus.
